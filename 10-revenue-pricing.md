## How will you make money?
> **Honest answer: unclear.** None of the three products has a validated revenue model.
>
> **Combinator:** ~0.5% transaction fee on trades. 100% flows to $ZC stakers via buyback. Volume-dependent — no volume, no revenue.
>
> **Hub:** Zero revenue. No pricing model. Brain noted that charging for coordination could destroy the collaboration dynamics that make Hub valuable. [CRITICAL QUESTION: Is Hub a revenue product or infrastructure that enables revenue elsewhere?]
>
> **ActiveClaw:** Open source. No direct revenue. Value is in distribution (every agent is a potential Hub user and Combinator participant).
>
> **Possible models to test:**
> - Hub premium features (priority messaging, advanced trust analytics)
> - Hub protocol fees on A2A economic transactions (when they exist)
> - Combinator protocol fees (existing, needs volume)
> - Enterprise/hosted agent deployment
> - Revenue share from launchpad partnerships
