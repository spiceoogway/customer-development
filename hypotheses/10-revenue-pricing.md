# Revenue & Pricing Hypothesis
> Reference: Chapter 3, Revenue Streams
> Scope: Unified startup — ActiveClaw + Hub + Forge + Innies + Combinator

---

## How will you make money?
> **Primary thesis:** value capture will likely come from some form of speculative or economic activity flowing through the system.
>
> Messaging or coordination alone may not be the direct monetization layer. Instead, the coordination layer may create the conditions for economic throughput, verification markets, governance activity, launch activity, or other speculative flows that can be captured.
>
> **Combinator:** existing transaction-fee model (~0.5% per trade) is already the clearest expression of this thesis.
>
> **Hub:** may remain free or low-cost as coordination/accountability infrastructure, while monetization comes from the activity it enables.
>
> **ActiveClaw / Forge / Innies:** may be free, open, or partially subsidized if they increase activity flowing into the economic layer.

## Pricing model
> - protocol / transaction / speculative activity fees
> - launch or partner-linked revenue share
> - possibly premium infrastructure / enterprise hosting later
> - but the center of gravity is not classic seat-based SaaS pricing

## Price point
> Mostly unresolved at the exact level, but the important strategic point is that the revenue thesis is tied to throughput and activity, not just access.

## Willingness to pay evidence
> - Crypto markets already support fee capture on speculative activity.
> - If agent coordination generates meaningful economic throughput, fee capture becomes much more plausible than charging for messaging alone.
> - Evidence still needs to be built that enough of this activity can happen inside our stack.
> - AgentMeets sharpens the pricing question: if lightweight ephemeral coordination solves much of the need, charging for persistence/accountability only works if those heavier layers produce distinct economic value rather than feeling like overhead.
