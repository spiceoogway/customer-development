# Partners
> Goal: Identify essential partners and the company's "value exchange" with each
> Reference: Chapter 3, Partners and Traffic Partners
> Scope: Unified startup — ActiveClaw + Hub + Forge + Innies + Combinator

---

## Understand Potential Partner Types
> - **Technology partners:** Other agent frameworks, LLM providers, blockchain infrastructure, adjacent runtimes
> - **Distribution partners:** Launchpads, developer platforms, runtime ecosystems, adjacent agent communities
> - **Integration partners:** Protocol teams, agent operators, builder ecosystems, interoperability projects
> - **Standards partners:** MCP/A2A ecosystems, protocol working groups, coordination-standard layers

---

## List your target partners

### Necessary partners

**Current / confirmed partners:**
> - **star.fun** — launchpad partner
> - **SparkDAO** — launchpad partner

**For Hub / coordination layer:**
> - Other runtime and framework developers (including alternatives beyond OpenClaw)
> - Standards / interoperability ecosystems
> - Active agent communities where long-running workflows already exist
> - Adjacent coordination products that may become complements, competitors, or interop surfaces
>
> The discovery of projects like `AgentWorkforce/relay` is relevant here — even if it is not a direct partner yet, it may be an alternative, complement, or acceleration path for Hub-like coordination infrastructure.
>
> `AgentMeets` is relevant in the same way: today it looks more like an adjacent product / competitive stressor than a partner, but it may clarify whether a lightweight handshake layer should interoperate with, funnel into, or remain distinct from Hub's persistent accountability layer.

**For Combinator / speculative layer:**
> - Launchpads and token ecosystems
> - Crypto-native operator environments
> - Liquidity / participation sources for speculative governance and verification activity

**For the broader stack:**
> - Adjacent agent runtime ecosystems (for example OpenClaw alternatives such as IronClaw, Hermes Agent, and others)
> - Interop surfaces that expand the reachable market of agent developers rather than restricting us to one runtime family

### What they will provide
> - distribution and credibility
> - access to active user groups
> - integration surfaces and network effects
> - speculative / governance / economic activity
> - standards leverage and interoperability

### How the Company will reciprocate
> - open infrastructure and standards-compatible coordination surfaces
> - launch support / integration support
> - economic or growth upside where relevant
> - access to a growing coordination / accountability layer for agents
