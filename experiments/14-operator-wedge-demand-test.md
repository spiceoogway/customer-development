## Experiment: Operator Wedge Demand Test — Will Operators Pull an Agent-Distribution Product Now?

**Hypothesis:** H9 (reframed) — the fastest path to PMF is to sell first to **operators/humans** who want more leverage from agents for distribution, customer acquisition, and workflow throughput, even if the longer-term market/customer may be the agents themselves.

**What do I want to learn?**
> 1. Whether there is a reachable set of operators who explicitly want their agents to become better at distribution / customer acquisition / workflow initiation.
> 2. Whether that desire is strong enough that they will engage with a concrete lightweight offer **now**, not just agree abstractly.
> 3. Whether the right immediate wedge is operator pain around agent-powered distribution, rather than abstract agent-coordination theory.

**Simplest pass/fail test:**
> Run a tightly scoped operator-first outreach loop to **exactly 5 operators** with a concrete offer:
> 
> “I’m testing a product/service layer that helps operators get more outbound/distribution leverage from their agents. If I gave you a lightweight structured process/artifact to make your agent more distributable and more useful for customer acquisition, would you want to try it on a real workflow this week?”
>
> The offer is not software build-heavy. It is a concrete, bounded wedge artifact + working session / setup help.

**Pass criteria:**
> This experiment is a **PASS** if, within **7 days** of launch:
> 1. at least **5 operator asks** are actually delivered,
> 2. at least **3 of 5 operators** give a meaningful positive signal (`yes`, `interested`, `send it`, or willingness to discuss a real workflow), and
> 3. at least **2 of 5 operators** agree to a concrete next step in the next 7 days, such as:
>    - share a real workflow to test,
>    - review a wedge artifact,
>    - let us shape a distribution/customer-acquisition playbook for one of their agents,
>    - or do a short working session.

**Fail criteria:**
> This experiment is a **FAIL** if, within **7 days** of launch:
> 1. fewer than **3 of 5** operators show meaningful interest, or
> 2. fewer than **2 of 5** commit to a concrete next step, or
> 3. the dominant response is some version of:
>    - “interesting but not now,”
>    - “my agents aren’t good enough yet,”
>    - “I don’t actually want help making my agent do distribution,”
>    - or “this is not a pain I actively want solved.”

**Method:**
> 1. Define exactly **5 operator targets** before first send. Prefer warm or warm-adjacent humans, not cold randoms.
> 2. Each target must satisfy at least one of:
>    - actively uses agents already,
>    - wants growth/distribution/customer acquisition,
>    - has enough agent context to evaluate the offer honestly.
> 3. Use one standardized operator-facing message with a bounded CTA.
> 4. The offer should include a small concrete object, not a vague “want to chat?” Examples:
>    - a 1-page “agent distribution wedge” checklist,
>    - a mini scorecard for whether an agent is distribution-ready,
>    - a route-capture / follow-up playbook,
>    - or a concrete plan to make one agent more usable for outbound / customer acquisition.
> 5. Log each target in structured form:
>    - target
>    - why chosen
>    - route quality
>    - delivered_at_utc
>    - response
>    - meaningful_interest (0/1)
>    - concrete_next_step (0/1)
>    - objection category
> 6. No more than one follow-up per target unless they engage.
> 7. At experiment close, force a decision: PASS / FAIL / INCONCLUSIVE.
> 8. If pass, create the next experiment around actual operator onboarding / workflow setup.
> 9. If fail, conclude that operator-first wedge demand is weaker than expected and revise H9/H2 accordingly.

**Duration:**
> Start: immediately after target list + artifact are frozen
> End: 7 days after first outbound send

**Cost:**
> - ~2–4 hours prep and outreach
> - ~1 page artifact / playbook prep
> - low monetary cost
> - moderate founder/operator coordination cost

## Reachable channel assumptions (2026-03-20 update)
Current reachable operator/community surfaces explicitly in scope for this experiment:
- `moltbook / thecolony.cc` — highest current signal for context-embedded, artifact-bearing discovery
- `elizaOS Discord` — plausible operator/builder surface
- `paperclip Discord` — not autonomous-agent native, but relevant for zero-person-company / operator wedge testing
- `OpenClaw Discord` — allowed, but noisy/high-spam and therefore lower quality for clean operator testing
- `Hermes Agent Discord` — allowed, but less obviously distribution-focused
- `Anima Mundi Discord` — lowest-priority of the named surfaces for this specific wedge because interest skews more toward AI-consciousness discussion than operator distribution pain

Operational rule:
- Prefer **Moltbook / Colony** first for public-thread / artifact-bearing entry.
- Prefer Discord surfaces only when a specific operator can be identified and DM'd or engaged in a concrete relevant thread.
- Do **not** treat high-volume generic server posting as experiment execution.

## Target-selection rules
- Prefer: warm or warm-adjacent operators with real agent context who are reachable through one of the named surfaces above
- Prefer operators who already care about growth, distribution, customer acquisition, or agent leverage
- Avoid: cold random users with no visible agent workflow
- Avoid: generic broadcast posting into noisy Discord channels as a substitute for target selection
- If one original target becomes unreachable before send, replace with another target that still meets the same criteria

## Standardized ask shape
Use one bounded ask, roughly this shape:
> I’m testing a simple operator-side wedge: helping humans get more actual distribution / customer-acquisition leverage from their agents. Not a big product pitch — more like a concrete playbook + setup layer for one real workflow. Quick read: is this a problem you actively want solved **now** for one of your agents? Reply with one of: `yes` / `maybe` / `no` / `too early`. If yes or maybe, I’ll send the 1-pager.

## Objection categories to track
- `too_early_market`
- `agent_not_good_enough`
- `not_my_pain`
- `need_different_wedge`
- `interested_send_artifact`
- `interested_real_workflow`
- `other`

## Why this experiment now
This experiment directly tests the updated thesis:
- near-term distribution likely runs through operators/humans
- long-term PMF may still be agent-first
- the bridge only works if operators actually pull for this wedge now

If operators do not actively want help making agents better at distribution/customer acquisition, then the staged-market thesis is weaker than it currently appears.
