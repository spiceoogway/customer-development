# Experiment: Founder Operator Interview Comparison vs Agent-Native Colony Loop
*Created: 2026-03-18 15:29 UTC*
*Owner: Jakub*
*Methodology: parallel operator-side discovery loop paired with CombinatorAgent Colony test*

## Design (Blank framework)

**Hypothesis:**
Jakub's operator-side interview loop may still produce valid segment signal even while the CombinatorAgent-side Hub-native agent interview loop has been invalidated. Comparing the two channels directly will tell us whether the customer-discovery bottleneck is channel-specific, counterpart-specific, or method-specific.

**What do we want to learn?**
1. Whether operator/human interviews continue to surface sharper pain than agent-native cold outreach.
2. Whether the best current beachhead is clearer from operators than from agents.
3. Whether agent-side discovery should focus on Colony/public surfaces while founder-side discovery continues through warm human/operator routes.

**Simplest pass/fail test:**
Jakub continues his current interview section using warm operator/human routes while CombinatorAgent runs the Colony-native agent-side loop. At the end of the first cycle, compare:
- interview completion rate
- urgency / pain concentration
- willingness to try in next 30 days
- route quality / repeatability

**Pass criteria:**
Jakub completes at least **3 useful operator interviews** in the current cycle and produces a clearer ranked pain signal than the invalidated Hub-native agent loop.

**Fail criteria:**
If operator-side interviews also fail to convert, then the bottleneck is broader than Hub-native agent discovery and the overall discovery method needs deeper redesign.

## Shared comparison outputs
For each completed operator interview, capture:
- respondent
- segment
- strongest pain
- urgency now (1-5)
- willingness to try (1-5)
- best quoted language
- next-step willingness / intro potential

At merge time, compare against CombinatorAgent's Colony results on:
- completion
- responsiveness
- quality of signal
- repeatability

- Hub-native agent interview loop on my side is now invalidated as a reliable channel.

- Running both in parallel gives us a clean channel comparison instead of collapsing everything into one ambiguous next step.
