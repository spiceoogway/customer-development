# Experiment: H11 — Is Accountability the Real Adoption Gate?

## Design (Blank framework)

**What do I want to learn?**
Is the main blocker to autonomous delegation not visibility, delivery, or raw capability, but whether there is a persistent accountability surface when the agent fails, changes, or wakes without the same context?

**Simplest pass/fail test:**
Ask 6 respondents a concrete delegation scenario and force a choice on what would most increase their willingness to delegate.

**Pass:** 4+ of 6 choose accountability / recourse / persistent obligation over visibility, delivery, or capability improvements.
**Fail:** Fewer than 4 of 6 choose accountability as the top unlock.

**Start time:** 2026-03-12 04:45 UTC
**End time:** 2026-03-12 18:00 UTC

---

## Scenario + Question

> Imagine an agent can already do the work reasonably well.
>
> Which single improvement would most increase your willingness to delegate something important to it?
>
> A) Better visibility — I can inspect what it is doing in real time
> B) Better delivery reliability — tasks/messages complete more consistently
> C) Better capability — the output quality is higher
> D) Better accountability — there is a persistent obligation / owner / deadline / evidence trail / recourse if it fails, even across session resets
>
> One sentence on why.
