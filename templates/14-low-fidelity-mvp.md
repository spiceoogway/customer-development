# Checklist 14: Build A Low Fidelity MVP
> Goal: Develop a low fidelity MVP to test customer interest
> Reference: Steve Blank, The Startup Owner's Manual, Chapter 4

---

## Purpose

Build the simplest live artifact that lets potential customers react to the value proposition without requiring a full product build.

The point is not polish. The point is to learn whether people care enough to engage.

---

## Low Fidelity MVP Options

Use one or more of the following:
- Splash / landing page
- Benefits summary
- Mock product page
- Signup / waitlist flow
- Demo or prototype
- Concierge / manual-service version
- Multiple MVP variants for comparison

---

## Minimum Website / Prototype Elements

A low fidelity MVP should usually include:
1. A clear headline
2. A short summary of benefits
3. A concrete call to action
4. A way to measure response

Optional additions:
- mock screenshots
- explainer copy
- pricing stub
- contact / signup form
- variant pages for different customer segments or messages

---

## Build Rules

1. Keep it simple and fast.
2. Do not overbuild before demand is proven.
3. Make response measurable from day one.
4. Treat every MVP as a pass/fail test, not a branding exercise.
5. If useful, create multiple low fidelity MVPs to compare positioning.

---

## Pass/Fail Planning

Before launch, define:
- Who should see this MVP
- What action counts as interest
- What response threshold counts as pass
- What response threshold counts as fail
- What qualitative follow-up you will run after initial responses

---

## Suggested Tracking

Track at minimum:
- visits
- source of traffic
- conversion to CTA
- replies / signups / meetings
- qualitative feedback
- strongest unexpected reactions

---

## Exit Criteria

- [ ] Simple live website or prototype exists
- [ ] The MVP is usable for testing customer interest
- [ ] A measurable call to action exists
- [ ] Pass/fail tests are identified before traffic is driven
- [ ] Response data can be collected and reviewed
