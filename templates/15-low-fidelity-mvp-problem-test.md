# Checklist 15: Low Fidelity MVP Problem Test
> Goal: Determine if people care about the problem the startup solves
> Reference: Steve Blank, The Startup Owner's Manual, Chapter 4

---

## Purpose

Use the low fidelity MVP to test whether the problem is important enough that real people respond.

This checklist is about validating problem interest, not just shipping a page.

---

## Traffic / Invitation Tactics

Invite potential customers to the MVP using one or more channels:
- Push tactics
  - direct outreach
  - messages to known prospects
  - operator-mediated intros
- Pull tactics
  - public posts
  - communities
  - social distribution
  - organic discovery
- Paid tactics
  - ads
  - sponsored distribution
  - promoted posts

Document which channels are used and what audience each one targets.

---

## Capture Mechanisms

The MVP should make it possible to capture signals of interest, such as:
- email collection
- signup / waitlist form
- request demo / request access
- reply button or contact path
- booking link

---

## Quantitative Measures

Track measurable response signals, including:
- web hits / visits
- conversion rate
- email captures
- CTA clicks
- time on site
- bounce / abandonment
- source of user / traffic source
- return visits

---

## Qualitative Follow-up

Use in-person or direct interviews where possible:
- ask what caught their attention
- ask what problem they thought the page was solving
- ask whether the problem is painful / frequent / urgent
- ask what they do today instead
- ask whether the value proposition scales beyond them to peers or team

---

## Interpretation Questions

After traffic and interviews, ask:
1. Did people care enough to act?
2. Which audience responded most strongly?
3. Which acquisition channel produced the highest-quality interest?
4. Did customers describe a real recurring problem, or just polite curiosity?
5. Does the feedback suggest a narrow niche or a scalable market?

---

## Pass/Fail Planning

Define before launch:
- target audience
- traffic source(s)
- pass threshold for response rate
- fail threshold for response rate
- what counts as meaningful interest
- what follow-up interviews will be run

---

## Exit Criteria

- [ ] Real potential customers were invited to the MVP
- [ ] Interest signals were captured (email, signup, meeting request, etc.)
- [ ] Response data was measured by source and behavior
- [ ] Direct feedback was collected through interviews or conversations
- [ ] Customer interest looks robust enough to justify further testing
- [ ] Response rate is high enough to support continued exploration
