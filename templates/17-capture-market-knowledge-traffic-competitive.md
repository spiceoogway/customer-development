# Checklist 17: Capture Market Knowledge, Traffic / Competitive
> Goal: Gain overall market understanding
> Reference: Steve Blank, The Startup Owner's Manual, Chapter 4

---

## Purpose

Build a grounded understanding of the broader market, not just individual customers.

This checklist is about understanding trends, competitors, traffic sources, business models, and the surrounding ecosystem well enough to position the startup intelligently.

---

## Learn from Market Participants

Meet and talk with people who have broad market visibility, such as:
- peers
- analysts
- journalists
- operators in adjacent companies
- domain experts
- ecosystem partners

Use these conversations to learn about:
- major trends
- key players
- market narratives
- unresolved customer needs
- where incumbents are weak
- what is changing quickly

---

## Research Areas

Research the market systematically, including:
- market trends
- category structure
- business models
- pricing approaches
- distribution channels / traffic sources
- important metrics
- tools and platforms used in the category
- emerging entrants
- substitute products
- adjacent categories that may converge with this one

---

## Competitive Analysis

Create structured views of the market:

### Competitive grid
Track competitors across dimensions such as:
- target customer
- core offering
- pricing model
- strengths
- weaknesses
- differentiation
- traffic / distribution model
- current traction signals

### Market map
Map the broader ecosystem:
- incumbents
- startups
- substitutes
- complements
- channels
- communities
- infrastructure providers
- talent clusters

---

## Events / Fieldwork

Attend relevant industry events where possible to:
- see product demos
- hear how companies position themselves
- observe what buyers react to
- identify strong operators and potential talent
- notice repeated problems or market confusion

---

## What to Capture

For each market-learning cycle, record:
- what sources were used
- what trends appear strongest
- which competitors matter most
- which customer needs remain unresolved
- where traffic and attention concentrate
- what business models seem to work
- what surprised us
- what strategic implications follow

---

## Interpretation Questions

After each round of market work, ask:
1. Do we understand the structure of the market well enough to position ourselves clearly?
2. Which traffic or distribution channels matter most?
3. Which competitors are real threats vs. superficial lookalikes?
4. Where are the biggest unresolved gaps in the market?
5. Is this market crowded, fragmented, or still forming?
6. Where does talent and momentum appear to be concentrating?

---

## Pass/Fail Planning

Use market knowledge to sharpen future tests:
- Which segments deserve deeper customer work?
- Which competitors should be tracked directly?
- Which channels should be tested first?
- Which claims or positioning angles should be validated with pass/fail experiments?

---

## Exit Criteria

- [ ] In-depth market understanding has been developed
- [ ] Key trends, players, and unresolved customer needs are documented
- [ ] Competitive grid has been created
- [ ] Market map has been created
- [ ] Important traffic / distribution patterns are understood
- [ ] Relevant industry events or equivalent field signals have been incorporated
- [ ] Pass/fail tests have been identified from the market knowledge work
