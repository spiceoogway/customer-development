# Checklist 16: Gain Customer Understanding
> Goal: Gain in-depth understanding of potential customers
> Reference: Steve Blank, The Startup Owner's Manual, Chapter 4

---

## Purpose

Develop a detailed, grounded understanding of how target customers actually live, work, decide, spend, and experience pain.

The goal is to replace abstract customer theories with direct observation and specific evidence.

---

## Research / Customer Interview Areas

In interviews and research, seek concrete answers about:
- how they make money
- how they spend money
- their workflow
- recurring pain points
- current solutions / workarounds
- the costs of current solutions
- who they learn from
- what publications they read
- which communities they participate in
- which people or influencers they trust
- what events they attend
- what language they use to describe their problems

Focus on specifics from recent real behavior, not opinions about hypothetical futures.

---

## Immersion Tactics

Go beyond interviews where possible:
1. Spend a day doing what customers do
2. Observe the workflow directly
3. Attend customer events, meetups, or industry gatherings
4. Become a customer yourself if possible
5. Use the same tools, channels, and environments they use

The point is to understand the world from inside the customer’s workflow, not just from outside commentary.

---

## What to Capture

For each target customer segment, capture:
- daily workflow steps
- where time is lost
- where money is lost
- where trust breaks down
- decision criteria
- buying triggers
- switching costs
- emotional language around pain
- frequency and severity of the problem
- signals that the problem is urgent enough to act on

---

## Report Card / Analysis Process

Maintain a repeatable analysis process after interviews and immersion work.

For each learning cycle, record:
- who was observed or interviewed
- when and where it happened
- what was learned
- what surprised us
- what contradicted prior assumptions
- which hypotheses were strengthened or weakened
- what pass/fail tests this suggests next

---

## Interpretation Questions

After each round of learning, ask:
1. Do we understand the customer’s real workflow in enough detail to predict where our product would fit?
2. Are the pain points urgent, expensive, frequent, or embarrassing enough to matter?
3. Are customers already spending time or money to solve this problem?
4. Do different customers describe the same problem consistently?
5. What is still vague or inferred instead of directly observed?

---

## Pass/Fail Planning

Define tests that would show whether customer understanding is deep enough to act on:
- Can we describe the workflow end-to-end?
- Can we name current alternatives and costs?
- Can we identify who influences purchase or adoption?
- Can we predict what message or MVP would resonate?
- Can we derive concrete pass/fail experiments from this understanding?

---

## Exit Criteria

- [ ] In-depth customer understanding has been developed
- [ ] Research includes direct interviews and/or direct observation
- [ ] Workflow, pain points, current solutions, and costs are documented
- [ ] Important publications, communities, and influencers are identified
- [ ] A report card / analysis process exists for turning observations into learning
- [ ] Pass/fail tests have been identified from the customer understanding work
