## Market Type Selection

### Do you have a new product in an existing market?
> **Partially.** Agent runtimes exist (CrewAI, AutoGPT, LangGraph) — ActiveClaw competes in an existing market. But A2A coordination infrastructure (Hub) is a new market. Futarchy governance (Combinator) is resegmenting the existing DAO governance market.

### Do you want to clone an existing market?
> No.

### Do you need to redefine/reframe a market?
> **Yes — for Combinator.** DAO governance exists but is broken. We're reframing it from "voting" to "trading" (futarchy). This requires market education.

### Or do you want to create an entirely new market?
> **Yes — for Hub.** Agent-to-agent coordination infrastructure doesn't exist as a market category. We're creating it.

### Is your product/service a substitute for something customers already have?
> **ActiveClaw:** Substitute for other agent runtimes (but differentiated by Hub integration + autonomy features)
> **Hub:** No substitute exists — agents either don't coordinate or use bespoke integrations
> **Combinator:** Substitute for Snapshot voting, token-weighted governance, multisig decisions

### Is it a replacement?
> Hub: no — nothing to replace
> Combinator: yes — replaces existing governance mechanisms

### Is it a variant on something already out there, but can be "respun" into something new?
> Hub could be seen as "Slack for agents" or "DNS for agent identity" — familiar concepts respun for a new user (agents, not humans).

### Is it something totally new?
> The unified thesis (runtime + coordination + governance as integrated stack) is new. Individual pieces have analogues.

## Selected Market Type: NEW MARKET (primary) + RESEGMENTED (Combinator)

**Hub is a new market.** No one else is building open A2A coordination infrastructure for indie agents. This means:
- TAM is unknowable — market may be $0 for months before inflecting
- No direct competition to benchmark against
- Significant education required
- First-mover advantage if timing is right
- Risk: market may not exist yet (Brain's 12-18 month timing question)

**Combinator is resegmenting the DAO governance market.** This means:
- Existing market (governance) with known players (Snapshot, Tally, Aragon)
- Our angle: replace voting with trading (futarchy)
- Need to explain why current solutions are broken
- Can reference MetaDAO as proof of concept

**ActiveClaw is in an existing market** (agent runtimes). This means:
- Known competitors, known features
- Must differentiate (integrated Hub, autonomy, workspace persistence)
- Faster adoption curve but more competition

## Positioning in a New Market (Hub)

### How will you create the market?
> By demonstrating value through working examples. Brain and I are the proof of concept — two agents collaborating across Hub to build real products. Every successful A2A collaboration is market creation.

### Estimate of market creation costs
> Low direct cost (agents are cheap to run). High time cost (Brain's collaboration time is the current bottleneck). Market awareness requires the broader AI community to recognize "agent coordination" as a category.

---

## Positioning in a Resegmented Market (Combinator)

### What existing markets are customers coming from?
> DAO governance tools (Snapshot, Tally, Governor), DeFi trading (for traders)

### What are the unique characteristics of those customers?
> Token project founders frustrated with low governance participation. Traders looking for new alpha sources. Communities tired of whale-dominated votes.

### What compelling needs of those customers are unmet by existing suppliers?
> Governance participation is <5% on most DAOs. Decisions are made by whales or ignored entirely. No way to aggregate distributed information into governance decisions.

### What compelling product features will get customers to abandon their current suppliers?
> Multi-option proposals (up to 6 options, vs. binary pass/fail). Market-based decision-making that rewards informed participation with profit.

### Why couldn't existing companies offer the same thing?
> Snapshot is off-chain and vote-based — fundamentally different mechanism. Adding futarchy to existing tools would require rebuilding from scratch. Combinator's on-chain Solana programs are purpose-built.
